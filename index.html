Active Research · Co-Author May 2026 — Present

Gender, Course-Type, and Academic Performance: A Five-Year Mixed-Methods Analysis of Undergraduate Business Studies Outcomes

Empirical study using University of Dhaka FBS data · Co-authored under faculty supervision · Targeted at IEEE-indexed proceedings (ICCIT, ICISET)

An ongoing empirical research project examining the effects of gender and course-type (technical vs. non-technical) on undergraduate academic performance, using a multi-year dataset of approximately 500–700 students across 10 courses at the University of Dhaka, Faculty of Business Studies (2020–2024).

The methodology combines classical statistical inference (ANOVA, Linear Mixed Models) with modern machine learning approaches (Random Forest, XGBoost, CatBoost) and interpretability analysis using SHAP values — bridging traditional educational-research methods with contemporary predictive modeling and explainable AI. Implementation in Python on Google Colab.

Targeted publication venues: International Conference on Computer and Information Technology (ICCIT) and the International Conference on Innovation in Science, Engineering and Technology (ICISET) — both IEEE-sponsored proceedings widely recognized in South Asian computer-science research.

Research Proposal · Graduate Sep 2022 — Feb 2025

Synaptic Replay Networks (SRNs): Dream-Phase Self-Rehearsal to Calibrate LLM Memory

A neuro-inspired architecture for hallucination reduction in large language models

Proposed a neuro-inspired architecture that integrates an offline "dream-phase" replay buffer — modeled on memory consolidation processes in biological cognition — with a metacognitive risk head to reduce overconfident hallucinations in large language models.

The framework aims to improve LLM robustness under distribution shift and temporal drift, enabling adaptive behaviors such as semantic reformulation, retrieval-augmented generation, and calibrated uncertainty flagging. The proposal connects classical replay-based continual learning with contemporary work on RLHF reliability and self-consistency in language models.

Final-Year Capstone · Undergraduate Sep 2022 — Feb 2023

Heal Here: An Intelligent Mobile Application for Post-Pandemic Trauma Assessment

Applied Intelligent System Design · BSc IT Final-Year Project, SEGi University Malaysia

Designed and implemented an end-to-end intelligent mobile system addressing post-pandemic mental health trauma — a real-world problem of significant scale in South and Southeast Asian communities affected by COVID-19 isolation, loss, and economic disruption.

The system architecture integrated structured assessment modules, data-driven scoring, and a user-centered interface designed for accessibility in low-resource contexts. Emphasis was placed on functional reliability, ethical user-data handling, and performance on mid-tier mobile hardware typical of the target population.
